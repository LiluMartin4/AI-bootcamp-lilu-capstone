# Story Requirements: Expanded Main Plot Tree

## Core Premise
- The protagonist is a blacksmith living in a kingdom that borders an ancient forest and a cursed lake.
- The blacksmith must always be referenced with they/them pronouns.
- The central arc reveals that the lost princess was transformed into the giant sea monster in the forest lake.
- Most branches should loop back to the central arc.
- Some side branches should become extreme outcomes: prison time, strange wilderness events, criminal paths, and possible death endings.

## Economy and Gold Rules
- Starting gold: 50.
- Gold income paths:
1. Legal work at the forge.
2. Theft and crime.
- Legal work should provide steady lower-risk income.
- Theft should provide higher-risk income with consequences.
- Consequences for theft can include:
1. Fines.
2. Confiscated items.
3. Guard pursuit.
4. Prison time.
5. Execution risk in severe repeat offenses.

## Inventory and Discoverable Item Rules
- The player can find, buy, craft, steal, or receive key items.
- Every major area should contain at least 2 discoverable items.
- Discoverable items should unlock dialogue, safer routes, or alternate choices.

Required key items:
1. Royal Crest Token.
2. Silver Bell.
3. Lake Charm.
4. Hunter Rope.
5. Forged Spearhead.
6. Moonwater Flask.
7. Prison Lockpick.
8. Bandit Signet.
9. Princess Pendant Fragment.
10. Old Lullaby Sheet.

## Recurring NPC Interaction Requirements
The story must include frequent conversations with multiple recurring NPCs.

Required recurring NPCs:
- Queen Edrin: ruler who is desperate to recover the princess.
- Captain Brann: law-and-order figure tied to prison and guard branches.
- Archivist Sen: source of ritual and royal history.
- Court Mage Ilya: knows hidden details of the transformation.
- Tavkeeper Mora: rumor broker and social connector.
- Sailor Finch: witness to lake sounds and sightings.
- Poacher Rook: prison contact with hidden forest routes.
- Merchant Dala: market fixer with trade and smuggling links.
- Shrine Keeper Oren: keeper of lake lore and cure methods.
- Masked Stranger Vey: bandit recruiter and manipulator.

## Market District Requirements
The capital market is a major story hub where the player can talk, buy, sell, investigate, and steal.

### Required Stores (At Least 10, here are 12)
1. Ember Anvil Forge Supplies.
2. River Salt Fishmonger.
3. Moonthread Clothier.
4. Lantern and Wick Chandlery.
5. Ironroot Herbalist.
6. Crown Ledger Pawn and Trade.
7. Three Crows Curios.
8. Dawnbread Bakery.
9. Mapmaker's Nook.
10. Stable and Tack House.
11. Bell and Brass Repairs.
12. Whisper Alley Contraband Stall.

### Market Interaction Rules
- Every store must sell at least 3 items.
- At least 4 stores must include optional theft opportunities.
- At least 5 store owners must have dialogue trees with relationship progression.
- Store prices can react to player reputation (honest, neutral, criminal).
- Market clues must feed into the princess-lake mystery.

## Story Tree (Longer Branching Structure)

### Node 0: Opening at the Kingdom Forge
The blacksmith starts before sunrise in the forge quarter. The street is loud with cart wheels, bells, and shouting merchants. They are handling a rush of orders while apprentices bicker over coal quality and steel temper. Rumors of the missing princess are everywhere, and customers keep repeating a new claim: roars have been heard from the forest lake at night.

A royal messenger arrives with sealed orders. Three other people interrupt almost immediately: a guard requesting repairs, a merchant demanding priority service, and a frightened child insisting they heard a woman singing from the direction of the woods.

Player options:
1. Close the forge and ride to the castle immediately.
2. Stay and complete paid work for extra gold.
3. Go to the market first for rumors and supplies.
4. Visit the guard barracks for official reports.
5. Follow tavern gossip before deciding.

Branch results:
- 1 -> Node 1 (Royal Mission)
- 2 -> Node 2 (Forge Income Branch)
- 3 -> Node 3 (Market Hub Branch)
- 4 -> Node 4 (Guard Branch)
- 5 -> Node 5 (Tavern Branch)

---

### Node 1: Royal Mission (Main Arc)
At court, Queen Edrin asks the blacksmith to discreetly investigate the lake and recover proof of what happened to the princess. Captain Brann monitors the conversation, while Mage Ilya appears uneasy when rituals are mentioned. The queen provides travel papers, a crest token, and hazard pay promise.

Player options:
1. Leave for the forest immediately.
2. Request access to the archives first.
3. Request a guard escort.
4. Question Mage Ilya in private.

Branch results:
- 1 -> Node 6 (Forest Road)
- 2 -> Node 7 (Archive Research)
- 3 -> Node 8 (Escort Trouble)
- 4 -> Node 9 (Mage Secrets)

---

### Node 2: Forge Income Branch
The blacksmith chooses money first. They can take simple jobs, difficult commissions, or questionable requests. Better workmanship earns reputation and stable income; rushed shortcuts can earn quick cash but create problems later.

Income examples:
- Basic repairs: low gold, low risk.
- Weapon commissions: medium gold, medium time.
- Secret lockwork jobs: high gold, high legal risk.

Player options:
1. Work honest contracts all day.
2. Take expensive rush jobs for nobles.
3. Craft criminal tools for underworld clients.
4. Stop work and report to the palace by nightfall.

Branch results:
- 1 -> Node 1 (Royal Mission with lawful reputation)
- 2 -> Node 10 (Noble Favor Branch)
- 3 -> Node 12 (Theft and Crime Branch)
- 4 -> Node 1 (Royal Mission)

---

### Node 3: Market Hub Branch
The market is dense, noisy, and full of opportunity. The blacksmith can shop, steal, gather rumors, accept errands, and build relationships with merchants. Merchant Dala hints that someone has been buying ritual materials in bulk and moving them toward the forest.

Required interactions in this node:
- Talk to at least 4 named merchants.
- Buy at least 1 item.
- Discover at least 1 clue item.
- Present at least 1 theft opportunity.

Player options:
1. Buy travel supplies and healing items.
2. Ask shops about lake rumors and missing people.
3. Attempt theft from a high-value stall.
4. Accept a legal courier job for gold.
5. Meet an underworld contact after dusk.

Branch results:
- 1 -> Node 6 (Forest Road with better prep)
- 2 -> Node 7 (Archive Research lead)
- 3 -> Node 12 (Theft and Crime Branch)
- 4 -> Node 13 (Courier Side Quest)
- 5 -> Node 15 (Bandit Contact)

---

### Node 4: Guard Branch
Captain Brann shares reports: strange tracks near the lake appear partly human and partly monstrous. Several witnesses vanished. The blacksmith can cooperate with law enforcement or manipulate the system.

Player options:
1. Join sanctioned patrol.
2. Request prison access to question poacher Rook.
3. Bribe guards for restricted evidence.
4. Reject oversight and go alone.

Branch results:
- 1 -> Node 8 (Escort Trouble)
- 2 -> Node 14 (Prison Side Quest)
- 3 -> Node 12 (Theft and Crime Branch)
- 4 -> Node 6 (Forest Road)

---

### Node 5: Tavern Branch
At the tavern, Mora and Finch describe hearing the lake creature cry in a human voice. A masked stranger named Vey offers a shortcut through dangerous territory. A miner sells a pendant fragment allegedly found near the forest edge.

Player options:
1. Trust Finch's map.
2. Follow Vey's shortcut.
3. Buy pendant fragment.
4. Start a tavern fight.
5. Return to the queen with rumors.

Branch results:
- 1 -> Node 6 (Forest Road)
- 2 -> Node 15 (Bandit Contact)
- 3 -> Node 16 (Pendant Clue Branch)
- 4 -> Node 14 (Prison Side Quest)
- 5 -> Node 1 (Royal Mission)

---

### Node 6: Forest Road (Main Arc Convergence)
The blacksmith enters the old forest, crossing abandoned camps and broken shrine stones. The wind from the lake carries strange echoes that resemble court lullabies. Routes split toward shrine ruins, hunter trails, and the open shore.

Findable item examples here:
- Hunter Rope.
- Old Lullaby Sheet.

Player options:
1. Investigate shrine route.
2. Follow tracks to the lake.
3. Camp and observe overnight.
4. Search abandoned camps.
5. Return to market for better supplies.

Branch results:
- 1 -> Node 17 (Shrine Revelation)
- 2 -> Node 18 (First Monster Encounter)
- 3 -> Node 19 (Night Attack)
- 4 -> Node 20 (Traveler Clues)
- 5 -> Node 3 (Market Hub Branch)

---

### Node 7: Archive Research
Archivist Sen uncovers records of a failed ritual binding a royal soul to lake spirits. Notes mention a crest, a bell tone, and a lullaby rhythm that may calm the transformed victim.

Player options:
1. Copy notes and leave for the forest.
2. Share findings with Mage Ilya.
3. Sell copied notes for fast gold.
4. Burn the notes in fear.

Branch results:
- 1 -> Node 17 (Shrine Revelation)
- 2 -> Node 9 (Mage Secrets)
- 3 -> Node 3 (Market Hub Branch)
- 4 -> Node 22 (Bad Ending: Ignorance)

---

### Node 8: Escort Trouble
The escort fractures after hearing roars near the lake. One guard flees. Another refuses to continue. The blacksmith must command, persuade, or abandon the group.

Player options:
1. Continue alone.
2. Persuade guard to continue.
3. Threaten guard into obedience.
4. Retreat to city and regroup.

Branch results:
- 1 -> Node 6 (Forest Road)
- 2 -> Node 6 (Forest Road with aid)
- 3 -> Node 23 (Escort Mutiny)
- 4 -> Node 3 (Market Hub Branch)

---

### Node 9: Mage Secrets
Mage Ilya admits the royal family attempted a hidden transformation ritual. They offer a Lake Charm and ask for silence. The blacksmith can cooperate, blackmail, or expose the truth.

Player options:
1. Accept charm and keep secret.
2. Demand full confession.
3. Expose mage publicly.
4. Threaten mage for gold.

Branch results:
- 1 -> Node 17 (Shrine Revelation)
- 2 -> Node 24 (Political Side Quest)
- 3 -> Node 14 (Prison Side Quest)
- 4 -> Node 12 (Theft and Crime Branch)

---

### Node 12: Theft and Crime Branch
The blacksmith steals for rapid wealth: shop tills, warehouse crates, or courier satchels. Success yields quick gold and rare goods. Failure triggers guards, bounty escalation, and imprisonment risk.

Player options:
1. Pickpocket a wealthy courier.
2. Break into Three Crows Curios at night.
3. Join Vey in a warehouse heist.
4. Stop stealing and return to legal work.

Branch results:
- 1 -> Node 25 (Street Chase)
- 2 -> Node 26 (Curio Break-In)
- 3 -> Node 15 (Bandit Contact)
- 4 -> Node 2 (Forge Income Branch)

---

### Node 14: Prison Side Quest
If arrested, the blacksmith spends multiple days in prison. They meet poacher Rook, an imprisoned scholar, and a smuggler who each offer different escape methods and lore fragments.

Player options:
1. Serve sentence and gather information.
2. Escape through damaged stonework.
3. Trade forged lockpick for route map.
4. Betray cellmates for early release.

Branch results:
- 1 -> Node 6 (Forest Road, delayed)
- 2 -> Node 27 (Fugitive Path)
- 3 -> Node 20 (Traveler Clues)
- 4 -> Node 1 (Royal Mission, trust penalty)

---

### Node 17: Shrine Revelation (Main Arc)
Shrine Keeper Oren confirms that the lake monster carries the princess soul. The curse can be softened through ritual components and careful approach. Aggression increases odds of tragic endings.

Player options:
1. Approach lake with crest and lullaby.
2. Build forged spear trap.
3. Wait for moonrise ritual window.
4. Return to town for one last missing item.

Branch results:
- 1 -> Node 18 (First Monster Encounter, calm route)
- 2 -> Node 28 (Violent Confrontation)
- 3 -> Node 29 (Ritual Attempt)
- 4 -> Node 3 (Market Hub Branch)

---

### Node 18: First Monster Encounter
The giant sea monster rises from black water, showing royal-blue scale patterns and a broken pendant chain. It reacts to names, tones, and key relics.

Player options:
1. Speak the princess name.
2. Ring the Silver Bell.
3. Attack first.
4. Retreat and regroup.
5. Offer food and test response.

Branch results:
- 1 -> Node 30 (True Confrontation)
- 2 -> Node 30 (True Confrontation, safer)
- 3 -> Node 28 (Violent Confrontation)
- 4 -> Node 17 (Shrine Revelation)
- 5 -> Node 20 (Traveler Clues)

---

### Node 30: True Confrontation (Main Arc Climax)
The blacksmith confirms the monster is the transformed princess. The ending depends on alliances, items, reputation, and prior crimes. The blacksmith can attempt sacrifice, diplomacy, ritual coalition, or exile.

Player options:
1. Offer forged crest-blade to absorb curse.
2. Lead joint ritual with queen, mage, and shrine keeper.
3. Refuse sacrifice and leave curse untouched.
4. Remain at lake as lifelong guardian.

Branch results:
- 1 -> Ending A (Bittersweet Salvation)
- 2 -> Ending B (Hard-Won Restoration)
- 3 -> Ending C (Kingdom in Fear)
- 4 -> Ending D (Exile Guardian)

---

## Side and Ending Node Summary
- Node 10: Noble Favor Branch -> wealth and influence, loops to Node 1 or Node 3.
- Node 13: Courier Side Quest -> legal gold, loops to Node 6.
- Node 15: Bandit Contact -> outlaw income path, loops to Node 6 or Node 27.
- Node 16: Pendant Clue Branch -> safer options in Node 18.
- Node 19: Night Attack -> survival test, may return to Node 17 or death ending.
- Node 20: Traveler Clues -> major evidence, loops to Node 17.
- Node 22: Bad Ending (Cursed Ignorance).
- Node 23: Escort Mutiny -> prison or return to Node 6.
- Node 24: Political Side Quest -> support for final ritual, loops to Node 17.
- Node 25: Street Chase -> capture, escape, or injury.
- Node 26: Curio Break-In -> rare relic gain or arrest.
- Node 27: Fugitive Path -> can rejoin main arc or end in execution.
- Node 28: Violent Confrontation -> high chance of death or tragic ending.
- Node 29: Ritual Attempt -> partial success or catastrophic failure.

## Dialogue and Interaction Rules
- Every major story node should include at least 2 named NPC conversations.
- NPC dialogue should react to player wealth source (forge work versus theft).
- NPC dialogue should react to inventory discoveries.
- Conversation choices should include persuasion, deception, intimidation, and empathy styles.

## Pronoun and Narrative Rules
- The blacksmith must always be referred to with they/them pronouns.
- Never use he/him or she/her for the blacksmith.
- Keep narrative focus on branching choices that repeatedly converge on the lake-princess arc.
